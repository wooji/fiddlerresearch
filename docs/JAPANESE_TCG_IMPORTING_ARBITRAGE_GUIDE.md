## 6. Customs & Duties Deep Dive

### HTSUS Classification & Duty Rate

**Applicable Code**: **HTSUS 4901.91.00** — "Printed materials; other printed matter... (trading cards, other)"

**Duty Rate**: **0%** (free entry)

**Rationale**: Trading cards are classified under printed books/publications, which carry zero import duty. This applies to all TCG products (Pokemon, MTG, One Piece, Digimon, etc.).

**Key Caveat**: Duty = 0%, but **customs processing fee** = typically 0-2% of declared value (CBP assessment surcharge for entry clearance; varies by port).

### US Customs De Minimis Threshold

**For Trading Cards**: **$800 USD equivalent per package**

**What This Means**:
- Single booster box (¥10,000 = $67) → **no customs duty, no processing fee**
- 10 booster boxes (¥100,000 = $670) → **no customs duty, no processing fee**
- 15 booster boxes (¥150,000 = $1,000) → **EXCEEDS threshold** → full entry documentation required (invoice, packing list, importer details)

**Practical Rule for Resellers**:
- Import up to $800 declared value per shipment → automatic pass-through (no CBP examination)
- Anything above = requires customs broker involvement ($150-300 fee for clearance)

### Value Declaration Best Practices

**What NOT to Do** (invokes CBP inspection + penalties)
- Under-declare value (e.g., declaring ¥10,000 box as ¥4,000) → flagged as fraud; CBP can assess 20% penalty + confiscation
- Declare "gifts" or "samples" when buying for resale → intent to evade duty is trademark of fraud

**What TO Do** (safe practice)**:
1. Use invoice from Mercari/Suruga-ya as DECLARED VALUE (proxy services auto-fill)
2. Mark "Merchandise" (not "Sample" or "Return") on customs form
3. Include packing list with item counts + individual pricing if >$800
4. Declare full landed cost (item + shipping + proxy fees) if over threshold

**CBP Inspection Red Flags** (trigger detailed exam, 5-7 day delay):
- Declared value significantly lower than typical market price for product (e.g., ¥10,000 box declared as ¥2,000)
- Shipment includes mixed conditions (sealed + loose singles) → suspicious of authenticity
- Frequent shipments from same seller (pattern of commercial resale) → registration as importer required
- No tracking numbers or proof of insurance in shipment documentation

**What Triggers Inspection** (from CBP playbook):
- Value >$800 (by rule, not discretion)
- Shipment shows signs of tampering (damaged tape, misaligned labels)
- Declared contents mismatch packing list
- Sender has prior fraud flags (CBP tracks repeat offenders)

## 7. Authenticity & Counterfeit Risk

### How to Spot Fake JP Sealed Boxes

#### **Red Flags on Packaging (Pre-Purchase)**
1. **Holofil Pattern**: Authentic JP booster boxes show uniform, fine-line holographic pattern at extreme angle. Counterfeits show:
   - Blotchy/uneven foil distribution
   - Missing reflectivity in corner regions
   - Foil that looks printed (flat) vs embossed (3D depth)
   - **Test**: Photo at 30° angle; authentic should show rainbow gradient sweep

2. **Text Clarity**: 
   - Authentic: Crisp, sharp logo text + set symbol (black ink edges laser-precise)
   - Counterfeit: Soft edges, slight registration offset (CMYK layers not aligned), visible dot-matrix pattern under magnification

3. **Corner Wear**: Authentic sealed boxes show ZERO corner crease/white stress marks (pristine manufacturing). Counterfeits often have pre-creased corners (sign of rough handling in shipping from fake factory).

4. **Barcode & Serial Number**:
   - Authentic JP boxes: 13-digit barcode with clean laser printing; serial number embossed (raised text)
   - Counterfeit: Printed barcode (flat, sometimes faded); serial number is flush/printed

5. **Shrink Wrap Thickness**: Authentic uses 0.5mm PVC heat-shrink (firm, uniform). Counterfeits use thin 0.2-0.3mm (loose, crinkled).

#### **Red Flags During/Post-Purchase**
6. **Pack Composition**: Counterfeit booster packs sometimes contain:
   - Misaligned foil artwork (off-center character image)
   - Wrong card stock weight (noticeably flimsy vs authentic)
   - Duplicate cards in single pack (should be 10-11 unique cards)

7. **Price Signal**: If eBay listing is >30% below market median for sealed box → **LIKELY COUNTERFEIT OR MISLABELED**. Honest sellers don't undercut by that margin.

```
  Declared value:                       $403 (< $800 de minimis, no duty)
  Customs processing (included in DHL): $0
  
STEP 6: FINAL LANDED INVENTORY
  Total received cost (COGS):           $403.36
  Per-unit cost:                        $80.67
  
STEP 7: RESALE PRICING
  Current eBay median (SV4.5 JP):       $70-75
  Realistic sell price (85% of median): $62
  GROSS MARGIN:                         $62 - $80.67 = -$18.67 per box ❌ LOSS

```

**Conclusion**: SV4.5 arbitrage does NOT work as quick flip (current eBay prices too low). Only viable as speculative hold for appreciation bet (requires 12-18 month patience + conviction in scarcity thesis).

## 11. Key Takeaways for Reseller System

1. **JP Pokemon Premium**: Real and structural (post-2023 simultaneous releases); premium 10-25% vs EN MSRP for scarcity sets. NOT a discount market.

2. **Import Arbitrage Reality**: 80% of commodity TCG imports (standard booster boxes, recent releases) **result in net loss** after shipping + fees. Only viable for:
   - JP-exclusive sets (<3M units sealed)
   - 5+ box consolidation (amortizes per-box shipping)
   - 12+ month hold thesis (appreciation gamble)

3. **Sourcing Hierarchy**: Suruga-ya (best reliability) > Amazon Japan (convenience) > Mercari (lowest price, higher risk) > Buyee (high fees, best service).

4. **Customs/Duty**: 0% duty on cards; $800 de minimis threshold is real (no processing for ¥100k+ shipments); value declaration best practice = invoice-based, no under-declaring.

5. **Counterfeiting**: 1-2% risk on Mercari (if high-feedback seller); <0.1% on Suruga-ya/Amazon JP. Holofil + barcode detail = primary distinguishers. BGS/PSA slabbing available in Japan (faster turnaround now).

6. **One Piece Opportunity**: OP imports slightly better than Pokemon (JP 10-18% premium vs EN, better sustained), but still requires consolidation + hold thesis to pencil.

7. **Other TCGs**: Vanguard, Digimon, DBS imports **not viable** — JP not at premium, EN demand weak, shipping costs kill margins.

8. **JPY-USD Headwind**: Current ¥148:$1 (JPY strength 2024-2026) compresses arbitrage margins 5-10% vs prior year. Forward rate hedging not practical for small resellers.
