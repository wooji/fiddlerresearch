## Deep Dive: Reprint Mechanics

### Why Reprints Matter

Reprints are the **primary wealth destroyer** in sealed investing. A set appreciating 100% can collapse 40–60% on reprint announcement. Understanding each TCG's reprint policy is critical.

### Pokemon: Quarterly Reprints (Predictable But Aggressive)

**Cadence:**
- Standard sets reprint 3–6 months post-release (Sword & Shield, Scarlet & Violet)
- Reprints are announced on Pokemon Company official website (~2–4 weeks before street date)
- High-print sets (like Crown Zenith) can see 5–8 reprints in a 2-year window

**Price Impact:**
- Post-release: +40–80% appreciation (3–6 months)
- Reprint announcement: -20–30% price drop (panic selling)
- Post-reprint flood: Consolidation at 1.2–1.5× retail (steady floor)
- Long-term: Sealed appreciation stops; new reprints devalue past sealed stock

**Investor Strategy:**
- **MUST flip quickly (3–6 months max)** before reprint announcement
- **Exit immediately on reprint news**
- **Don't hold >12 months** unless set has been reprinted already (scarcity proven)

**Examples:**
- Sword & Shield (Dec 2019) → $120 MSRP → $200 (6mo) → reprint announced Mar 2020 → drops to $150 → reprints continue → eventually settles $100–120
- Shining Fates (Feb 2021) → $100 MSRP → $300 (12mo, no reprints pre-announcement) → repro-announced → drops to $200 → reprints continue → 2026 sealed floor $280–350

---

### Lorcana: Ad-Hoc Reprints (Unpredictable, Rare)

**Cadence:**
- No quarterly cadence; reprints are announced on an as-needed basis
- Fabled (May 2025) saw first major reprint announcement (Jun 2026, after 13 months)
- Ravensburger policy: "Reprints protect gameplay viability, not scarcity"
- No public roadmap for future reprint dates

**Price Impact:**
- Release to Month 12: Sustained appreciation (no reprints announced)
- Reprint announcement: **Extreme spike (panic-buy)** then crash
  - Fabled example: $247 (Mar 2026) → $837 (Jun 2026, reprint announced) → $250 (Oct 2026, reprints hit)
- Post-reprint flood: Sealed originals still trade at premium (2–3×) vs. reprint products

**Investor Strategy:**
- **Buy strong IP at release; hold 6–12 months**
- **EXIT immediately on reprint announcement** (before panic-spike collapses)
- **Don't buy into spikes** (Fabled spike was anomaly; current $250 is more realistic)
- **Monitor Ravensburger quarterly statements** for reprint policy changes

**Examples:**
- First Chapter (Aug 2023) → $120 MSRP → $400+ appreciation → no reprints announced in 34mo → currently $275 (2.29× retail) — UNUSUAL FOR SEALED
- Fabled (May 2025) → $120 MSRP → $837 spike (reprint announcement) → current $250 (post-reprint consolidation)

---

### MTG: Reserved List Hedge + Predictable Annual Reprints

**Cadence:**
- Reserved List (~10% of cards ever printed) never reprints = eternal scarcity floor
- Standard sets reprint annually in core sets; supplemental sets rarely reprint
- Policy announced 30+ years ago; no changes expected

**Price Impact:**
- Reserved List cards: Guaranteed 200%+ appreciation over 10–20 years
- Standard sets: Appreciation stops after 2 reprints; floor is usually 1.2–1.5× retail
- Collector booster boxes (premium format): Hold value better than standard (lower print run, higher inherent value)

**Investor Strategy:**
- **BUY Reserved List booster boxes for long-hold** (20+ years)
- **Flip standard sets quickly** (3–6 months before reprints announced)
- **Collector booster > standard booster** (rarer format, better appreciation)
- **Watch Reserved List prices on secondary** (TCGPlayer daily updates)

**Examples:**
- Original Modern Masters (2013) → $150 MSRP → appreciation stalled at $250–350 (reprints in 2015, 2017) → current ~$350 (steady floor, no reprints since 2017)
- Collectors' Edition (1990s) → original MSRP $40–60 → current $500–1500+ (30-year uninterrupted appreciation)

## Liquidity Analysis: How Fast Can You Sell?

Liquidity is critical for resellers with working capital constraints. Long sell-times = money locked up.

### Pokemon Booster Box (Strongest Liquidity)

- **TCGPlayer listed items:** 1000+ at any time (e.g., Crown Zenith booster boxes)
- **Sell speed:** <6 hours at market price; <1 hour at 5% discount
- **Bid-ask spread:** 1–3% (tight market)
- **Alternative channels:** eBay, Whatnot auctions, local shops, Discord communities
- **Capital turnover:** 3–4 boxes/week per reseller is achievable

**Verdict:** Fastest liquidity. Money available immediately upon sale.

---

### Lorcana Booster Box (Good Liquidity, But Slower)

- **TCGPlayer listed items:** 50–200 depending on set (smaller market than Pokemon)
- **Sell speed:** 24–72 hours at market price; 1–2 weeks at asking price
- **Bid-ask spread:** 2–5% (wider than Pokemon)
- **Alternative channels:** eBay, PriceCharting, local collectors, Reddit r/lorcana
- **Capital turnover:** 1–2 boxes/week is typical

**Verdict:** Good liquidity but slower than Pokemon. 3–5 days average time-to-cash.

---

### MTG Booster Box (Moderate Liquidity, Format-Dependent)

- **TCGPlayer listed items:** Varies by format and age
  - Modern Masters (collectors): 50–100 (rare, slower)
  - Standard booster boxes: 200–500 (faster)
- **Sell speed:** 24–48 hours at market price for standard; 1–2 weeks for collectors
- **Bid-ask spread:** 2–5%
- **Alternative channels:** CardKingdom, Scry Fall, local shops, competitive communities
- **Capital turnover:** 1–2 boxes/week (slower than Pokemon)

**Verdict:** Moderate liquidity. Format matters (standard faster than limited edition).

## Risk Assessment: Downside Scenarios

### Worst-Case Scenario 1: Market Recession

**Impact:**
- Discretionary collector spending drops 30–50%
- Secondary sealed prices fall 20–40% across all categories
- Liquidity slows 50–75% (fewer buyers, wider bid-ask spreads)

**Mitigation:**
- Pokemon vintage holds best (proven 20-year resilience)
- MTG Reserved List holds best (collector tier, exclusive value)
- Lorcana most vulnerable (new category, collector base smaller)

**Action:** Diversify across all 3. Don't over-allocate to Lorcana in uncertain economy.

---

### Worst-Case Scenario 2: TCG Fades from Fashion

**Impact:**
- Sealed boxes trade at 0.8–0.9× retail long-term
- Market consolidation; smaller publishers forced out
- Collector base shrinks 20–30%

**Probability:** Low (30+ year categories like Pokemon/MTG have proven resilience)

**Mitigation:** Focus on vintage/Reserved List (proven to appreciate regardless of hype cycles)

---

### Worst-Case Scenario 3: Ravensburger Changes Lorcana Reprint Policy

**Impact:**
- Reprints become quarterly (like Pokemon)
- Sealed appreciation stops; sets revert to retail within 12 months
- Lorcana sealed investing becomes unviable

**Probability:** Medium (policy is 3 years old, could shift)

**Mitigation:** Exit all Lorcana holdings if reprint cadence accelerates. Monitor quarterly announcements.
