KEY FINDINGS (TL;DR)
================================================================================

1. LORCANA IS A SHORT-HOLD, IP-DRIVEN PLAY
   - Sealed appreciation: 45-129% ROI possible on strong IP
   - Hold window: 6-12 months optimal; avoid >18 months
   - Exit rule: Sell at 1.5-2× retail or 50-100% ROI, whichever comes first
   - Reprint risk: Ravensburger reprints infrequently but unpredictably

2. IP STRENGTH DETERMINES SUCCESS
   ✓ BUY: Maleficent (First Chapter $275 at 34mo, +129%), Ursula ($190 at 25mo,
          +58%), Frozen/Winterspell ($174 at 10mo, +45%)
   ✗ SKIP: Jafar, Aladdin, Rapunzel, generic ensemble themes

3. PRODUCT WINDOW IS TIGHT
   - Peak scarcity: 0-6 months post-release
   - Stabilization: 6-18 months
   - Decline: 18-24 months (EOL, newer sets cannibalize inventory)
   - Obsolescence: 24+ months (Into Inklands $90 at 28mo, -28% ROI)

4. FORMAT MATTERS: BOOSTER BOX ONLY
   - Booster box ($120-145 MSRP): Investable; all appreciation data applies
   - Illumineer's Trove ($49.99): Retail ±5%, no appreciation
   - Gift sets, single packs: Volatile or retail-fungible; avoid

5. REPRINT POLICY IS THE COMPETITIVE MOAT
   - Ravensburger reprints rarely (vs. Pokemon's quarterly reprints)
   - Fabled was first major reprint event at 13 months (May 2025 → Jun 2026)
   - Reprint spike is anomaly: Fabled hit $837.50 on panic, now $250 (deflated)
   - No set has been reprinted twice; policy could change

6. VS. POKEMON: COMPARABLE SHORT-TERM, RISKIER LONG-TERM
   - 6-month ROI: Pokemon 40-80%, Lorcana 45-80% (tie)
   - 12-month ROI: Pokemon 60-120%, Lorcana 45-129% (Lorcana higher but volatile)
   - 20-year appreciation: Pokemon proven 300-1000%+, Lorcana unproven
   - Liquidity: Pokemon <6 hours, Lorcana 24-72 hours

  - Wilds Unknown (Nov 2025) → NO reprints announced at 7 months

COMPARISON TO COMPETITORS:
  Pokemon: Quarterly reprints (3-6 month window) — AGGRESSIVE
  Lorcana: Ad-hoc reprints (12+ month gap) — RARE
  MTG: Annual reprints + Reserved List protection — MODERATE

INVESTOR IMPLICATION:
  + Lorcana reprints less frequently → longer scarcity floors
  - Lorcana reprints unpredictably → must monitor announcements
  - Reprint announcement triggers panic → spike then crash (see Fabled)

ACTION PLAN:
  1. Monitor Ravensburger official website monthly for reprint announcements
  2. Exit immediately upon reprint news (don't wait for market to process)
  3. Don't buy into reprint spikes (Fabled $837 was one-time anomaly)
  4. Avoid sets >18 months old unless strong IP sustaining premium

PRICING MECHANICS: WHY SOME SETS HOLD, OTHERS COLLAPSE
================================================================================

STRONG IP FLOOR (Maleficent, Ursula, Frozen):
  - 1.5-2.5× retail sustained at 25+ months
  - Collector demand for character outweighs supply cycles
  - Sealed boxes held as collectible keepsakes (not cracked for singles)

WEAK IP FLOOR (Jafar, Rapunzel, generic adventure):
  - 0.8-1.0× retail within 12-14 months
  - No character appeal; commodity pricing kicks in
  - Sets trade at or below MSRP after initial hype fades

REPRINT IMPACT:
  - Spike (panic-buy): 3-5× retail for 2-4 weeks
  - Crash (reprint flood): -40% to -60% from spike, consolidates at 1.5-2.5×
  - Long-term: Sealed originals premium over reprints, but don't reach spike prices

PRODUCT WINDOW DECLINE:
  Month 0-6: Peak appreciation (0-60% gain)
  Month 6-12: Stabilization (40-100% gain possible)
  Month 12-18: Plateau (gains slow; newer sets compete for demand)
  Month 18-24: Decline (-10% to -30% common)
  Month 24+: Heavy discount (30-50% below peak; EOL phase)

DECISION MATRIX: BUY, HOLD, SELL
================================================================================

BUY:
  ✓ New release (0-3 months) + strong IP (Maleficent/Ursula/Frozen tier)
  ✓ Presale active + secondary showing 1.2-1.5× retail
  ✓ No reprint rumors
  ✓ Allocation: 1-2 booster boxes per set; MSRP budget only

HOLD:
  ✓ 6-12 months post-release, strong IP, <1.5× retail (still room to run)
  ✓ No reprint announcement issued
  ✓ eBay sold velocity >10/month
  ✓ Exit when: 50-100% ROI achieved OR 12-month mark reached

SELL:
  ✓ 100%+ ROI achieved (lock gains; reallocation capital)
  ✓ Reprint announced (exit immediately; price will crash)
  ✓ Weak IP apparent at 6-9 months (cut losses; redeploy)
  ✓ 18+ months old (EOL phase; new sets cannibalize inventory)
  ✓ Hitting MSRP-or-below at any age

AVOID:
  ✗ Sets >18 months old (except First Chapter/Ursula if already holding)
  ✗ Weak IP themes (Jafar, Rapunzel, generic ensemble)
  ✗ Illumineer's Trove, gift sets, single packs (no appreciation)
  ✗ Presale buys >30 days before release (early FOMO pricing)

================================================================================
LORCANA VS. POKEMON: KEY TRADE-OFFS
================================================================================

LORCANA ADVANTAGES:
  + Higher per-set ROI on strong IP (45-129% vs. Pokemon 40-80%)
  + Fewer reprint waves (1 per 3 years vs. Pokemon quarterly)
  + Lower entry price ($120 vs. Pokemon $120-130)
  + Less format complexity (booster box dominates)
  + Appeal to older collectors (Disney nostalgia, not gameplay)

LORCANA DISADVANTAGES:
  - Smaller secondary market (24-72 hour sell time vs. Pokemon <6 hours)
  - Unpredictable reprint policy (no official cadence)
  - Unproven category (2 years old; unproven 20-year hold)
  - IP-dependent (weak characters don't appreciate; strong ones plateau)
  - Policy shift risk (could mirror Pokemon reprints at any time)

POKEMON ADVANTAGES:
  + Proven 30-year track record
  + Excellent liquidity (<6 hours to sell)
  + Predictable quarterly reprints (plan flips 3-6 months out)
  + Vintage appreciation proven (300-1000%+ over 20 years)
  + Gameplay drives sustained floor demand

POKEMON DISADVANTAGES:
  - Aggressive reprint cycles (quarterly floods)
  - Must flip quickly (3-6 months before reprints announced)
  - Larger capital requirement per box ($120-130)
  - More format variants to track (ETB, collection box, etc.)

VERDICT:
  Lorcana = better for medium-term holds (6-12 months) on strong IP
  Pokemon = better for short flips (3-6 months) or long holds (10+ years)
